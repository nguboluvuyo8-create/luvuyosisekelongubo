How Deskwork fits into your day

  1. Draft, don't write from scratch. Use the Email Generator for client follow-ups, internal notes, or proposals — pick a tone, get a draft, edit to taste.
  2. Close the loop on meetings. Drop your raw notes into the Summarizer right after a call so action items don't get lost.
  3. Plan before the day plans you. List today's or this week's tasks in the Planner and get a realistic, prioritized order.

Responsible use of AI

Every draft on this site is a starting point, not a finished product. Outputs can be incomplete, generic, or factually off — read everything before you send, file, or act on it. Deskwork doesn't store your input anywhere or send it to a third party; all generation happens right in your browser.

About Deskwork

Deskwork bundles three everyday writing-and-planning tasks into one workspace: drafting emails, summarizing meeting notes, and building a prioritized schedule. It's built as a static site — every "AI" output is generated by rule-based logic running directly in your browser, styled to read like a clean first draft you'd get from an assistant.

How this differs from a connected AI model

This demo doesn't call an external AI API, so it won't understand nuance, context outside what you type, or anything outside its templates the way a model like Claude would. Treat every output as a structured rough draft, not a finished, fact-checked piece of writing.

Responsible AI use disclaimer

  • Outputs are generated automatically and may be incomplete, generic, or contain mistakes — always review before sending, filing, or acting on them.
  • Don't paste in sensitive personal information (ID numbers, banking details, medical information) — even though this demo processes everything locally, it's good practice for any AI tool.
  • This tool supports your judgment; it doesn't replace it. Final wording, decisions, and priorities are yours to confirm.
  • No data entered here is stored, logged, or shared — input lives only in your browser tab for this session.
